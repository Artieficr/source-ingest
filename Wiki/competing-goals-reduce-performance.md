# Competing Goals Reduce Performance

## Content
When multiple goals compete for the same limited resources -- time, attention, and mental energy -- performance on all of them drops, not just on some. One specific pattern from the research: when people are given both a quantity goal and a quality goal at the same time, they tend to sacrifice quality in favor of quantity. More broadly, people gravitate toward whatever task feels most completable rather than whatever actually matters most, hunting for easy wins while the genuinely important work gets pushed back and deferred repeatedly.

This dynamic also appears at the organizational level: research comparing companies shows that those with fewer strategic priorities significantly outperform companies juggling many priorities at once. The same dynamic plays out for individuals -- trying to run ten things simultaneously tends to produce some progress on everything but completion of nothing, including the things that should have been finished. The conclusion drawn is that not everything on a person's list needs to be treated as a priority; spreading effort across too many competing goals undermines all of them.
