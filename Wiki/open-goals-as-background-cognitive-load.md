# Open Goals as Background Cognitive Load

## Content
Every unfinished commitment, untouched goal, or to-do list item functions like an open background app on a computer: it doesn't sit inertly waiting to be revisited, it keeps running and consuming cognitive resources the entire time. Research on unresolved decisions shows they drain more cognitive energy than unfinished tasks -- meaning a goal you've told yourself you'll get to eventually, but haven't started, costs more mental bandwidth than if you had simply decided not to pursue it at all and closed that mental "task" for good.

The effect compounds with the number of open items. Someone juggling 15 things isn't only carrying that load when they sit down to focus on one of them -- their brain carries all 15 constantly, producing a steady, low-grade exhaustion that's hard to trace back to its source. This often shows up as feeling worn out before even starting a task, or burning out within minutes of beginning. People frequently attribute this feeling to laziness, but the more accurate explanation is simply having too many open commitments running in the background for too long, creating a sense that nothing is moving forward even when effort is being expended.
