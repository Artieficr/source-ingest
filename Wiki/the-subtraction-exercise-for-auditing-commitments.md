# The Subtraction Exercise for Auditing Commitments

## Content
This is a practical exercise for identifying which commitments deserve continued attention and which should be dropped. First, write down everything currently being done -- every habit, goal, system, and commitment made to oneself -- without filtering anything out, either on paper or in a notes app. Second, go through the list and mark which of these items have actually been done consistently over the last 30 days. Third, from that consistently-done subset, mark which ones have produced something real and noticeable -- better energy, better output, more happiness, or better health, something concrete that can be pointed to.

Whatever remains after both filters is what deserves extra focus and attention going forward. Everything else on the original list represents a cognitive tax being paid without any corresponding return. The exercise is paired with three reflective questions meant to surface candidates for removal: what's on your plate that isn't producing anything meaningful, what are you holding onto out of guilt rather than because it works for you, and what would actually happen if you simply stopped doing it. The suggested approach is to ask what can be removed before looking for anything new to add.
