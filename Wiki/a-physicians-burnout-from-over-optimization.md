# A Physician's Burnout From Over-Optimization

## Content
The presenter, a physician, describes going through a period during medical school and residency where he tried to optimize every aspect of his life simultaneously: sleep tracking, nutrition logging, reading goals he stated out loud, two separate workout protocols, meditation, journaling, and social media, all tightly optimized. Despite this intensity, he estimates he was operating at only about 40% effectiveness across all of it, his schedule was so loaded that he had almost no social life, and even when he was physically present with other people he was mentally preoccupied with optimizing his next move or his next social media post.

He attributes this partly to being surrounded by extremely high-performing peers in medicine, which created pressure to "match" their output by continually adding more to his routine -- taking ideas from books he read daily and adding them to an ever-growing list of things to implement. This eventually became severe enough that he passed out from exhaustion while working in a clinic, which he describes as a wake-up call. After that, he cut back to two or three things that actually moved the needle for him -- training consistently, protecting his sleep, and not touching his phone in the morning -- and says his output increased drastically once he stopped spending energy managing an overly complex system of habits and trackers.
