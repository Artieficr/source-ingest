# Focused Effort Compounds More Than Scattered Effort

## Content
People who execute well over long stretches of time -- not just talk about productivity or post about it -- share a common trait: they protect a small number of things with significant intensity, rather than spreading effort thinly across many goals. This isn't because they are less serious or ambitious than people juggling many commitments; it's because focused, sustained effort compounds far more over time than scattered effort that fizzles out, which tends to be more performative than productive.

The key additional ingredient is depth: completing one thing properly, then moving to the next, consistently, at meaningful depth, rather than touching ten things at a surface level that never move forward. Ten things done superficially and indefinitely will make a person feel busy without getting them where they want to be -- a pattern described as turning oneself into a robot that just gets through tasks rather than investing passionately in a smaller set of things, which produces a much larger increase in enjoyment and genuine progress.
